Frequently Asked Questions

Who do we want to help?

Students from low-income families, aged 10 to 16, who are interested in tech and programming.

How will mentoring be carried out?

Mentoring sessions will be carried out on Zoom, 1 hour per week, on a 1 mentor to 1 student basis

Can I help to mentor students?

Of course! We are looking for university students who are in a computer-related major, such as computer science, information systems, data science, etc. However, we are open to mentors from different backgrounds as long as you have the ability to teach students programming and are committed to making a difference in their lives! Contact us for any queries you may have :)

What is the commitment like as a mentor? What is the duration of the mentorship?

The mentorship will be across a twelve week period, from January 2023 to March 2023. Mentors should be able to commit minimally 1 hour per week (1 student), but may choose to take on more students if he or she wishes to commit more hours per week! If you wish to continue accompanying your mentee on his or her journey to become a self-directed learner in tech, you are more than welcomed to do so!

Do I need to come up with a curriculum as a mentor?

Nope, we will provide video tutorials for mentees to learn the basic concepts of programming. As a mentor, your role is to assist the mentee with difficulties that he or she faces as she completes the tutorials. If your mentee wants to learn beyond the suggested basic curriculum, you are encouraged to use free resources online to further your mentee’s learning.
